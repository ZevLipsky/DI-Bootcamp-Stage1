Wilbur and Orville Wright were American inventors and pioneers of aviation. In 1903 the Wright brothers achieved one of the first flights with a powered, sustained and controlled airplane; they surpassed their own milestone two years later when they built and flew the first fully practical airplane.

Here is a timeline of the Wright Brother's life

  • 1867- Wilbur is born on April 16 in Millville IN.
  • 1871- Orville is born on August 19 in Dayton OH.
  • 1886- Orville Starts a printing busniess while he is still in high school.
  • 1889- Orville begins to publish West side news.
  • 1892- Brothers open Wright Cycle Company.
  • 1894- Brothers start weekly magazine Snap Shots.
  • 1895- They begin to manufacture their own brand of Bicycles.
  • 1896-The Wrights take an interest in the "flying problem" after hearing of the flight of Samuel Langley's unmanned "Aerodrome" and the death of glider pilot Otto Lilienthal
  • 1899- Wilbur devises an aerodynamic control system for aircraft and builds a kite to test the concept.
  • 1900-1903- The Brothers Make Multiple trips to kitty hawk to conduct experiments of their flying machines.
  • 1903- December 17 The first successful flight is made! The flight was 12 seconds long and flies 120 feet.
  • 1905-The Wright brothers develop the first practical airplane and demonstrate it before a small audience. They offer their invention to the U.S. Army, but the Army is not interested.
  • 1906- U.S. Patent Office grants patent for the Airplane.
  • 1909The Wrights demonstrate a new airplane and the U.S. Army buys its first military aircraft. The Wrights begin to manufacture airplanes and teach pilots
  • 1910The Wright brothers open the first civilian flight training school in Montgomery, Alabama.
  • 1912- Wilbur dies of Typhoid Fever on May 30th in Dayton OH at the age of 45.
  • 1948- Orville dies of a heart attack on January 30th in Dayton OH at the age of 76.
